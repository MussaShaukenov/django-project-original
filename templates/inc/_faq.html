Coronaviruses are a large family of viruses that are common in both animals and humans.
The 2019 new coronavirus (COVID-19) is a virus that is a pneumonia-related disease first detected in Wuhan, China.
Many of those who became ill during the outbreak in Wuhan, China, had links to a large wholesale seafood and animal market, suggesting the initial infection was from wild animals.

Previously, coronaviruses have caused outbreaks of diseases, including:
- severe acute respiratory syndrome or SARS in 2002-2003, when more than 8 thousand people fell ill in 37 countries, of which more than 800 people died;
- Middle East respiratory syndrome or MERS. Between 2012 and January 2020, more than 2,000 people fell ill in 27 countries, of which 780 died.

Coronavirus can be transmitted from person to person by airborne droplets (coughing, sneezing, talking), and infection can also occur by contact (through household items) and alimentary (through contaminated food, water). The transmission factors are air, food, and household items.

According to Chinese scientists, the natural reservoir of the coronavirus 2019 (COVID-19) is wild animals (bats). The main source of human infection was the South China wholesale seafood market, where wild animals, including bats and reptiles, were purchased.

The coronavirus is unstable in the external environment, dies at a temperature of 57 degrees of C and above, is unstable to disinfectants and alcohol-containing agents.

The incubation period (from the moment of infection to the appearance of signs of the disease) is from 2 to 14 days, more often 2-7 days.
